How do you purchase new electronics without worrying about the environmental impact?

If you always thought using electronics saves trees and lowers the usage of paper, know that the reverse is actually true. Manufacturing and developing electronic devices has a serious impact on the socio-economic conditions and environment of the country. Each time a microwave, tablet, smartphone, or laptop leaves the factory, it will eventually add to the mounds of toxic e-waste that causes long-term damage to the earth.

Why We Do What We Do? Let’s Explore Some Real Facts

What do electronics
cost the earth?

Each time a new desktop PC is manufactured, the process consumes 1.8 tons of raw materials. A single PC consumes 10 times its weight in chemicals, rare metals, and other materials. Every chip requires 3.5 pounds of fossil fuels to build. More than 400 gallons of water are needed to produce a single desktop computer.

How are materials for
electronics sourced?

95% of the metals needed to manufacture electronics are sourced from eastern countries. The mining conditions are deplorable and statistics talk about disasters all the time. Developed countries can recycle plastics and metals like say, aluminum. However, only 30% is recycled in countries like the US. And, that’s just one example.

Where do electronics
end up?

By the time your devices reach their end-of-life cycle, they’ll contribute to the millions of tons of e-waste that ends up in massive dumps in developing countries in Asia and Africa. Statistics gathered by the World Economic Forum report that by the end of the year 2018, 48.5 million tonnes of e-waste was generated across the world.
